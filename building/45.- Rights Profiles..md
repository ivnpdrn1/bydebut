# Article VII — Rights Framework

## Section 7.1 — Purpose

The Rights Framework establishes the constitutional principles governing ownership, intellectual property, attribution, licensing, permissions, and economic participation within the byDebut ecosystem.

Its purpose is to ensure that every Living Story Universe evolves through collaboration without compromising the legitimate rights of creators, contributors, publishers, or future participants.

Rights exist to protect creativity while enabling responsible innovation.

---

# Section 7.2 — Constitutional Recognition of Rights

Every Living Story Universe originates from an authorized work.

That original work possesses constitutional rights that remain protected throughout its entire lifecycle.

The existence of derivative works shall never invalidate the rights of the original work.

Original rights remain permanent.

Creative evolution expands value.

It does not transfer ownership.

---

# Section 7.3 — Rights Holder

Every Story Passport shall identify one or more Rights Holders.

A Rights Holder may include:

- Author
- Publisher
- Literary Estate
- Organization
- Educational Institution
- Corporate Entity
- Government Organization
- Other legally recognized owner

The Story Passport is the official record of Rights Holder identity.

---

# Section 7.4 — Original Work

The Original Work is the constitutional foundation of every Living Story Universe.

The Original Work shall always retain:

- authorship
- attribution
- identity
- ownership
- historical precedence

No derivative contribution may claim to replace the Original Work.

The Original Work remains permanently recognized.

---

# Section 7.5 — Reader-Creator Rights

byDebut recognizes that readers may become creators.

Reader-Creators possess rights over the original contributions they introduce into a Living Story Universe.

These rights include:

- attribution

- contribution history
- portfolio recognition
- economic participation when authorized

Reader-Creator rights never diminish the rights of the Original Work.

Both rights coexist.

# Section 7.6 — Derivative Rights

Every Story Lineage constitutes a derivative creative work.

Derivative works possess their own creative identity while remaining connected to the Original Work.

Every derivative work shall preserve:

- Story Passport reference

- attribution

- contribution history

Derivative rights are governed by the permissions established in the Story Passport.

# CTO Recommendation (A Major Strategic Addition)

While writing this article, I realized there is one capability that could become a defining innovation of byDebut:

## **Rights Profiles**

Instead of every Story Passport manually configuring permissions, create reusable **Rights Profiles**.

Examples:

* **Open Collaboration**
* **Educational**
* **Commercial Publisher**
* **Research**
* **Family Legacy**
* **Public Domain Enhanced**
* **Film Development**
* **Children's Literature**
* **Corporate Knowledge**
* **Government Archive**

A Rights Holder could select a profile during Story Passport creation, and the platform would automatically configure licensing, AI permissions, chronology permissions, contribution rules, review workflows, and economic participation.

This would dramatically simplify onboarding while preserving flexibility, and it aligns perfectly with the constitutional principles established in this article. I would later document this as both a Product OS feature and a Domain Model concept because it will make the platform much easier to use at scale.
