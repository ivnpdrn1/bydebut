# The byDebut Lexicon

One of the strongest long-term assets we can build is a formal **Lexicon**.

Every new concept should meet four criteria before becoming official:

1. **It solves a real conceptual problem**, not just a naming problem.
2. **It is intuitive enough that authors and publishers will naturally adopt it.**
3. **It supports the Story Operating System architecture.**
4. **It can remain meaningful as the platform expands beyond books into films, games, education, and other media.**

This discipline prevents us from inventing terminology that is clever but not useful.

# The Next Strategic Evolution: From Canon to Standards

I think we've now reached the point where byDebut should begin publishing not only its own vocabulary, but also **its own standards**.

Examples:

* **Story Passport Standard (SPS)** — what information every Story Passport must contain.
* **Story Lineage Standard (SLS)** — how provenance, genealogy, and rights are represented.
* **Story DNA Standard (SDS)** — how narrative intelligence is structured for AI interoperability.
* **Creative Attribution Standard (CAS)** — how human and AI contributions are transparently recorded.

If these standards are well designed, they could eventually be adopted beyond byDebut itself. That would move the company from being merely a software provider to becoming the steward of an emerging industry standard.

In my view, **that** is the path toward building not just a successful product, but a company capable of defining an entire category.
