# Purpose

This document defines how stories evolve inside byDebut.

A Story Lineage is a traceable creative branch that emerges from an authorized Story Passport.

No Story Lineage may exist independently.

Every Story Lineage must preserve:

- origin
- authorship
- attribution
- permissions
- chronology
- relationship to the Canon
- relationship to previous versions

---

# Definition

A Story Lineage is an authorized evolutionary path inside a Living Story Universe.

It may represent:

- continuation
- alternate ending
- alternate beginning
- alternate chronology
- character perspective
- educational adaptation
- cinematic adaptation
- translation
- simplification
- expansion
- experimental version

A Story Lineage is not a disconnected copy.

It is part of the official creative graph of a Story Passport.

---

# Core Rule

Every Story Lineage must belong to exactly one Story Passport.

The Story Passport governs the Lineage.

The Lineage expands the Story Universe.

---

# Required Identity Fields

Every Story Lineage must contain:

- Lineage ID
- Story Passport ID
- Parent Lineage ID
- Parent Version ID
- Creator ID
- Title
- Description
- Creation Date
- Status
- Visibility
- Relationship Type

---

# Lineage Status

A Story Lineage may have the following statuses:

- Draft
- Private
- Public
- Under Review
- Featured
- Canon Candidate
- Canonical
- Archived

Status determines how the Lineage appears inside the Story Portal.

---

# Relationship Types

A Story Lineage may relate to its parent through one or more of the following relationship types:

- continuation
- branch
- fork
- remix
- adaptation
- translation
- chronology shift
- character perspective
- genre transformation
- educational version
- cinematic version
- simplified version
- expanded version

The relationship type must be explicit.

---

# Chronology Rules

A Story Lineage may modify chronology only if allowed by the Story Passport.

Possible chronology operations include:

- reorder events
- insert events
- remove events
- create alternate timelines
- create parallel timelines
- merge timelines
- split timelines
- revisit past events
- create prequels
- create sequels

Every chronology change must preserve traceability.

---

# Attribution

Every Story Lineage must preserve:

- original author
- rights holder
- Reader-Creator
- AI assistance
- editor
- reviewer
- translator
- contributor history

Attribution cannot be removed.

---

# AI Participation

AI may assist in creating a Story Lineage only within permissions defined by the Story Passport.

AI contributions must be recorded.

AI may not become the author of record.

AI may not override human approval when approval is required.

---

# Canon Relationship

Every Story Lineage must clearly state its relationship to Canon.

Possible Canon relationships include:

- Non-canonical
- Inspired by Canon
- Canon-compatible
- Canon Candidate
- Approved Canonical Extension

The Canon always remains preserved.

---

# Forking

A Story Lineage may be forked if the Story Passport permits it.

Forking creates a new Lineage with:

- new creator
- new Lineage ID
- parent reference
- inherited attribution
- inherited permissions
- new contribution history

Forking never deletes or overwrites the parent.

---

# Version History

Every Lineage may contain multiple versions.

Each version must preserve:

- timestamp
- creator
- change description
- AI involvement
- editorial involvement
- previous version reference

Version history is permanent.

---

# Visibility

A Story Lineage may be:

- Private
- Unlisted
- Public
- Community Only
- Rights Holder Only
- Enterprise Only

Visibility must respect Story Passport permissions.

---

# Review

A Story Lineage may require review before publication.

Review may include:

- AI review
- community review
- editorial review
- rights holder review
- educational review
- professional review

Review requirements are defined by the Story Passport.

---

# Publication

Publishing a Story Lineage creates a permanent event.

The platform must record:

- publication date
- publishing user
- Story Passport
- parent Lineage
- attribution
- AI participation
- visibility
- review status

Publication must never erase previous history.

---

# Metrics

A Story Lineage may accumulate:

- reads
- likes
- comments
- forks
- reviews
- Creative Capital
- Narrative Resonance
- Story Gravity contribution
- community ranking

Metrics may influence discovery but must not replace editorial judgment.

---

# Technical Requirements

Every backend service, frontend component, AI workflow, API, and analytics system must preserve the Lineage relationship graph.

No Lineage may become orphaned.

No Lineage may bypass the Story Passport.

No Lineage may erase attribution.

---

# Technology Independence

The Story Lineage Standard is independent of any database, framework, AI model, or cloud provider.

The implementation may change.

The standard remains permanent.

---

# Relationship to Other Documents

This document defines how stories evolve.

The next standard, **Story_DNA_Standard.md**, defines how the platform understands the narrative identity of a Story Universe.

---
