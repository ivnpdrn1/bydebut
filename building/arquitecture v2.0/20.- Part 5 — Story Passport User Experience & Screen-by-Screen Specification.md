# CTO Strategic Recommendation

Ivan, while writing this section, I realized something that I think is even more important than any screen layout.

We have been talking about **Story Passports** and **Story Portals** as if they were pages.

I no longer think they are.

I believe **the Story Portal is the digital twin of a book**.

That means every physical or digital book has a corresponding living digital counterpart that evolves independently while remaining permanently linked to the original work.

This concept is much broader than "a page with information."

It opens possibilities such as:

* Live events.
* Author Q&A sessions.
* Community milestones.
* Educational paths.
* Adaptation announcements.
* Reading clubs.
* AI-assisted discussions.
* Film production updates.
* Merchandise.
* Future editions.

In other words, the Story Portal becomes the **living digital presence** of a book throughout its lifetime.

I believe this concept has the potential to become one of byDebut's defining innovations. Rather than treating books as static products with a landing page, we would treat them as evolving digital entities with identities, histories, communities, governance, and continuous interaction. That is a much more powerful vision—and one that aligns perfectly with the Living Story Economy™ we have been designing.
