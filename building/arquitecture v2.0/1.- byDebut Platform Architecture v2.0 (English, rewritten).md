# Executive Summary

byDebut is **not** an AI writing application.

It is **not** a book editor.

It is **not** a fan-fiction website.

byDebut is the **Story Operating System (Story OS)** that enables rights holders to transform books into **Living Story Universes**, where readers become creators inside an authorized, traceable, and monetizable ecosystem.

The original work always remains protected.

Every derivative work remains connected to its origin.

Every creative contribution increases the value of the entire ecosystem.

---

# Vision

To transform every published book from a static product into a continuously evolving creative platform.

Books should no longer end on the final page.

They should become living universes capable of growing through their communities.

---

# Mission

Empower authors, publishers, and rights holders to unlock the creative potential of their readers while protecting intellectual property and creating new economic opportunities for everyone involved.

# Intellectual Property Philosophy

byDebut exists to strengthen intellectual property, not weaken it.

The platform never encourages unauthorized copying.

Every derivative work:

* references the original work,
* preserves attribution,
* respects permissions,
* remains fully traceable,
* operates within rights defined by the rights holder.

## CTO Recommendation (Version 2.0)

After rewriting this document, I believe we have reached a turning point in the project.

**This document should now become the "constitution" of byDebut.** Every technical decision—database schema, frontend flows, AI orchestration, pricing, onboarding, and investor messaging—should trace back to these principles.

From this point on, I recommend that every new feature answer three questions before it is built:

1. **Does it increase the value of the Story Passport?**
2. **Does it strengthen the Reader-Creator Economy?**
3. **Does it create measurable value for rights holders?**

If the answer to all three is "yes," it belongs in the platform. If not, it should wait for a future release.

In my view, this is no longer just an MVP architecture—it is the blueprint for an entirely new category in digital publishing.
