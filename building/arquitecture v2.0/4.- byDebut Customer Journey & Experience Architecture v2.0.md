# byDebut Customer Journey & Experience Architecture v2.0

## Designing the World's First Reader-Creator Ecosystem

**Version:** 2.0
**Status:** Product Experience Blueprint
**Audience:** Product, UX, Engineering, Marketing, Investors, Publishers, Gemini XPRIZE Judges

---

# Executive Philosophy

Most digital products optimize for **user acquisition**.

byDebut optimizes for **ecosystem creation**.

The objective is not to convince millions of anonymous visitors to sign up.

The objective is to enable every Story Passport to become a self-growing ecosystem that continuously attracts, converts, and retains participants.

The customer journey therefore begins **before a user visits byDebut**.

It begins when an author or publisher decides to transform a book into a Living Story Universe.

---

# The Five Customer Journeys

Unlike traditional SaaS platforms, byDebut has **five primary journeys**, each with a different objective.

1. Rights Holder Journey
2. Reader-Creator Journey
3. Publisher Journey
4. Producer Discovery Journey
5. Community Growth Journey

Together, they form one interconnected ecosystem.

---

# Journey 1 — Rights Holder

## Primary Persona

* Independent Author
* Publisher
* Literary Agency
* Rights Holder

## Mission

Transform an existing book into a Living Story Universe.

---

### Stage 1 — Discovery

The rights holder learns that byDebut can extend the commercial life of a book.

Primary message:

> **"Your book doesn't end on the last page."**

Primary CTA:

> **Create Your Story Passport**

---

### Stage 2 — Evaluation

Questions they ask:

* Is my copyright protected?
* Who owns derivative works?
* Can I control permissions?
* Can I monetize participation?
* How does this increase book sales?

The platform answers these before asking for registration.

---

### Stage 3 — Onboarding

The onboarding is centered on one outcome:

**Create the Story Passport.**

Workflow:

```text
Create Account
↓

Create Organization
↓

Verify Rights Ownership
↓

Upload Manuscript
↓

AI Story Analysis
↓

Generate Story DNA
↓

Create Story Passport
↓

Generate Official QR

↓

Launch Living Story Universe
```

Notice:

The book is not uploaded.

A Story Universe is launched.

---

### Stage 4 — Launch Dashboard

The first dashboard should not show analytics.

It should answer one question:

> **How do I activate my readers?**

Primary actions:

* Download QR
* Generate launch assets
* Invite readers
* Create first campaign
* Share Story Passport

---

### Stage 5 — Growth

The dashboard evolves into a business console.

KPIs:

* Reader Activation
* New Lineages
* Community Size
* Returning Creators
* Reader Retention
* Story Evolution Score

---

# Journey 2 — Reader-Creator

This is the most important experience in the platform.

The reader should never feel they are entering a software application.

They should feel they are entering **the universe of the book they just finished.**

---

## Entry Point

The journey begins with:

* QR Code
* Official Link
* Publisher Campaign
* Author Invitation

Never:

Generic homepage.

---

### First Screen

Instead of:

"Create an account"

The screen says:

---

**You have just completed this story.**

Now imagine…

What if it continued?

Welcome to its Living Story Universe.

---

One button.

> **Enter the Story Passport**

---

### Story Passport

The reader immediately sees:

* Original Story
* Author
* Community
* Active Lineages
* Featured Variations
* Reader Discussions
* Story Timeline

No menus.

No dashboard.

Only the story.

---

### The First Creative Moment

This is the most critical screen in the entire platform.

Never ask:

> "Write a new version."

Instead ask:

> **What would you change?**

Examples:

* Another ending
* Different protagonist
* Save a character
* Different historical period
* More romance
* More mystery
* A movie adaptation
* Continue the story

This removes creative anxiety.

---

### AI Collaboration

The user does not start from an empty page.

The AI responds:

> "Great. I'll preserve everything except the ending."

The first creative success should happen in less than five minutes.

---

### Creator Identity

After publishing the first lineage, the reader receives a Creator Profile.

Not just an account.

A creative identity.

---

# Journey 3 — Publisher

Publishers see a completely different product.

Their homepage begins with:

Portfolio Overview.

Questions answered:

* Which books are growing?
* Which communities are active?
* Which lineages are becoming popular?
* Which books need campaigns?

The publisher manages ecosystems, not files.

---

# Journey 4 — Producer Discovery

Studios don't search by title.

They search by signals.

Example:

"Show Living Story Universes where readers strongly prefer alternative endings."

Results include:

* Story Passport
* Community metrics
* Lineage popularity
* Character engagement
* Narrative evolution

This transforms byDebut into a discovery engine.

---

# Journey 5 — Community Growth

Communities become self-organizing.

Every Story Passport becomes a living network.

The platform recommends:

* readers,
* creators,
* reviewers,
* editors,
* discussions,
* challenges,
* events.

The goal is sustained activity, not one-time visits.

# Retention Strategy

Traditional SaaS relies on notifications.

byDebut relies on evolving stories.

Users return because:

* someone continued their lineage,
* their version gained readers,
* the author responded,
* a challenge started,
* a lineage became featured,
* a producer bookmarked their work.

The story itself becomes the notification.

# Strategic Observation: The Next Evolution

Ivan, after writing the last four documents, I think we've reached another important inflection point.

We are no longer designing **a product**.

We are designing **a category**.

The next documents should stop describing individual features and start describing **the operating model of the company itself**.

I recommend that the next master document be:

> **byDebut Ecosystem Operating Model v2.0**

This will answer questions that investors inevitably ask:

* How does the ecosystem govern itself?
* How are rights, permissions, and trust managed?
* How are communities moderated?
* How are derivative works reviewed?
* How does AI interact with humans?
* How are disputes resolved?
* How does the platform scale from 1,000 Story Passports to 10 million?
* What are the network effects and governance mechanisms?

This document would become the blueprint for operating byDebut as a global platform, not merely launching it as an application. In my opinion, it is the missing piece between the strategic vision we've defined and the technical implementation we've already begun in Supabase and will soon continue in the Workflow Engine.
