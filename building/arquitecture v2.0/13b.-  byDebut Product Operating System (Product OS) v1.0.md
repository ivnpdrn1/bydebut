# Phase 1: Product Execution

From this point forward, our work should become **execution-driven**, while the Company Brain evolves only when we discover something strategically important.

I recommend organizing Phase 1 into six execution tracks that run in parallel:

### Track 1 — Story Operating System (Core Backend)

Complete the Workflow Engine, AI orchestration, event bus, permissions, and Story Passport lifecycle.

### Track 2 — Business Portal

Build the experience for authors, publishers, rights holders, and publicists.

### Track 3 — Story Portal

Build the immersive experience for Reader-Creators entering through Story Passports.

### Track 4 — AI Editorial Organization

Implement every AI agent, orchestration logic, prompt assembly, memory, evaluation, and human approval workflow.

### Track 5 — Living Story Economy

Implement subscriptions, billing, campaigns, referrals, Story Challenges, Creative Capital, Story Gravity, and analytics.

### Track 6 — Growth & Ecosystem

Launch QR activation, publisher onboarding, partnerships, developer APIs, educational pilots, and the first Living Story Universes.

## One final strategic recommendation

I believe the Company Brain should now become a **version-controlled repository**, separate from the application code.

In practice:

* One repository for the **Company Brain** (strategy, standards, architecture, governance).
* One repository for the **Product OS** (React, Next.js, Supabase, AI, infrastructure).

This separation mirrors the distinction between **thinking** and **building**. It allows the company's strategic knowledge to evolve independently from the implementation while ensuring that every engineer, AI agent, and future employee always starts from the same authoritative source. In my opinion, this will be one of the strongest organizational decisions we can make for byDebut's long-term success.
