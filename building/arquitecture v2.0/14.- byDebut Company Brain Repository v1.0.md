# Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)

One practice I strongly recommend adopting is **Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)**.

Every major decision should be captured in a short document that explains:

* **Context** вЂ“ What problem were we solving?
* **Decision** вЂ“ What did we decide?
* **Alternatives Considered** вЂ“ What other options were evaluated?
* **Consequences** вЂ“ What are the expected benefits and trade-offs?

For example:

* ADR-001: Why byDebut is a B2B2C platform.
* ADR-002: Why Story Passport is the central entity.
* ADR-003: Why Reader-Creators enter through Story Passports instead of uploading books.

This preserves institutional memory and prevents revisiting the same debates years later.

# How AI Will Work

This is where I think byDebut can become truly unique.

Every AI agent should receive two layers of context:

## Layer 1 вЂ“ Permanent Context

Loaded from the Company Brain:

* Mission
* Constitution
* Product OS
* Canon
* Standards

This changes infrequently.

---

## Layer 2 вЂ“ Runtime Context

Loaded dynamically:

* Story Passport
* Story DNA
* Story Lineage
* User request
* Retrieved narrative context (RAG)
* Current workflow state

This changes with every request.

This separation keeps AI behavior consistent while allowing each interaction to be tailored to a specific Story Universe.

The Company Brain should evolve beyond documentation.

In the future, it should become a **knowledge graph**.

The Company Brain should answer by linking:

* The Platform Architecture section.
* The Business Model rationale.
* The Rights Framework.
* The Product OS implications.

That is organizational intelligence, not just documentation.

# My Final Recommendation Before We Resume Coding

I would establish one formal rule for the project:

> **No feature is implemented until it can be traced back to the Company Brain.**

Every pull request, Claude Code prompt, architectural decision, and product enhancement should reference the section of the Company Brain that justifies it.

That discipline creates something much larger than good software. It creates a company whose strategy, product, engineering, AI systems, and future employees all operate from the same coherent foundation. In my experience, that level of alignment is extremely rareвЂ”and it can become one of byDebut's strongest long-term competitive advantages.
